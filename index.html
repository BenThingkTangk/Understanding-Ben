Brain and body

Why I work for days, then sleep for days

Autism + CTE + depression Kidney issues Chronic back pain

When I am in a “work mode” window, my brain locks in and I can push very hard for long stretches. That is when I build products, write code, and try to move everything forward while the focus is there.

After that, my system crashes. Between autism, depression, CTE, kidney problems, and back pain, the only way my body can reset is by shutting down and sleeping a lot. Those weekends where it looks like I disappear are not me being lazy; they are my nervous system and organs trying to repair enough so I can make it through the next push.

Emotional load

Grief, isolation, and why weekends are “system rest”

Losing Coco, having no close friends around, and not having money to go anywhere turned weekends into something very different for me. If I am not working, there is not much to distract me from how empty and depressing things can feel.

So I use weekends as system rest: sleep, recover, hide a bit, and try not to sink too deep mentally. It can look like I am avoiding everyone, but most of the time I am just trying not to fall apart.

How to read my behavior

What it means when I…

  • I am racing the clock while my brain is online. It is me trying to create a future where things are more stable for all of us.
  • My system has hit its limit. I am not ignoring you; I am trying to get enough back to function.
  • Talking, being told what to do, or being corrected can feel like too much when I am overloaded. I pull back to avoid snapping or shutting down.
  • It is not disrespect. My brain is juggling health, money, deadlines, and pain, and sometimes the little things fall out of my head.
